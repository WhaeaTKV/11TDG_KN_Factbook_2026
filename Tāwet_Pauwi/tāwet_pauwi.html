Tāwet Pauwi


The Tāwet Pauwi is an average sized sea bird most often found in the Bay Of Plenty or Hauraki Gulf.
They nest offshore islands all round the North Island and, like the NZ Shag stick around fishing boats
and eat their scraps. They are dark brown with tube noses and broad wings, and their calls are said to
resemble a cat fight. There nesting sites are mainly clear of any predators, but they are often accidentaly
caught by commercial fishers leaving there numbers at less than 12,000 pairs.

The Tāwet Pauwi is a species of shearwater (a kind of seabird), and there is some evidence that shearwaters
have a symbiotic relationship with the Tuatara. Although it is not confirmed some scientists believe that
Tuatara and these seabirds will share a burrow, and while the seabirds leave the nest the Tuatara will
protect the eggs from any insects that may come and invade the nest, at the same time getting a meal out of
the insects. Although this symbiotic relationship has not yet been confirmed, researchers A. J. GASTON and
P. SCOFIELIY published a paper saying that the seabird and Tuatara populations on North Brother Islad in the
Cook Strait do in fact coexist, often living in the same burrows
