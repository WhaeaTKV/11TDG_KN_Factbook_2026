pukekos!


  • Pukeko's are also known as the swamp hen.

  • Though native to New Zealand, they're also widespread in numerous oversea countries.

  • Their main physical characteristsics include an indigo body, black wigs and head, white feathers underneat their tails and bright red bills, legs and feet.

  • When disturbed pukekos tend to turn up their tails.

  • Their diets consist of grasses, clover, berries, seeds, insects and eels.

  • Pukeko's usual call is a squawk or hiccup.

  • Swampy and grassy areas are the favoured habitats of pukekos.

  • Pukeko's are good swimmers because they have webbed feet.

  • Although thought of to have a fairly cautious and suspicious personality, pukeko's are generally friendly birds.
