It comprises four subspecies: the nominate subspecies is found in the North Island, South Island, and Auckland Islands; the obscura subspecies is found in the Three Kings Islands; and the oneho subspecies inhabits the Pulnets Islands. This species is listed in authoritative catalogs such as Avibase, Clements List, and HBW/BirdLife. The New Zealand honeylover is 17-20cm in length. The male has yellowish-green plumage with a purplish metallic sheen on its head and neck, while the female has a less pronounced bluish-green sheen on its head. At dawn, it emits a call consisting of 2-6 bell-like notes . It mainly inhabits native forests such as Podocarpus macrophyllus forests and urban green spaces. The breeding season is concentrated from September to January of the following year. The female buildthe ground to a height of 16 meters, and the incubation period is approximately 13-15.5 days.
